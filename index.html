Violent Crime Trend Comparison






When we look at the overall violent crime trends across Malaysia, the data reveals an obvious decline in violent crimes from 2000 to 2022. While crimes such as bodily injuries and rape saw peaks around 2010, the subsequent reduction can be attributed to enhanced law enforcement efforts and a push for community-driven safety initiatives.

By 2022, these efforts seem to have paid off, as violent crime figures dropped significantly, reflecting the positive outcomes of Malaysia's sustained focus on reducing violence.




Intentional Homicide?




This stream graph tracks intentional homicide rates by state from 2014 to 2022, and paints a more detailed picture of how homicide cases evolved in the country.

In 2016, we see the beginning of a steady decline across multiple states. However, by 2019, there’s a sharp increase in key states like Johor and Selangor, signaling potential systemic challenges.

In 2020, a notable drop occurs, likely influenced by the pandemic, which led to fewer interactions and possibly less criminal activity. By 2022, however, a significant spike is observed, suggesting the post-pandemic rebound and the possible impact of socio-economic factors.
